    Overview¶

    • Cloud — Top-level category covering all parts of the ECCCH.
      • Data — Information and datasets managed in the cloud.
        • Digital asset — Digitized materials representing heritage content.
          • Photograph — Still image digital assets.
          • Video — Video recording digital assets.
          • Document — Text-based digital assets.
          • 3D model — Three-dimensional digital representations.
          • Instrumental measurements — Recorded measurements from instruments.
        • Knowledge graph data — Structured semantic data linking heritage entities.
      • Semantic resource — Conceptual and linguistic resources.
        • Ontology — Formal conceptual models of a domain.
        • Vocabulary — Controlled lists of terms and definitions.
        • Taxonomy — Hierarchical classifications of concepts.
        • Semantic mapping — Correspondences between different schemas or vocabularies.
        • Thesaurus — Semantic networks of related terms.
      • Software — Applications and software systems.
        • Closed-source application — Proprietary software tools.
        • Open-source application — Openly licensed software tools.
      • Infrastructure and services — Underlying hardware, system resources and digital services.
        • Data service — Services providing access to datasets.
        • Compute service — Services offering computation capabilities.
        • Storage service — APIs managing data storage operations.
      • Community service — Support and engagement resources for users.
        • User support service — Help and assistance for platform users.
        • Training service — Learning and capacity-building activities.
        • Workshop service — Interactive events for collaboration and learning.
      • Workflow — Defined sequences of processes and tasks.
        • Text-based workflows — Workflows documented as textual instructions that are executed manually, providing basic knowledge sharing but limited reproducibility and automation [D3.2].
        • Script-based workflows — Script based workflows that encode execution logic and can be reused across compatible environments, improving portability, versioning, and partial reproducibility [D3.2].
        • Graph-based Workflows — Fully executable and orchestrated workflows managed by the cloud, enabling end to end automation, deep provenance tracking, and federated execution across multiple nodes [D3.2].
